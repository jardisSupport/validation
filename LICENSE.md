## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I use Jardis in a commercial product?**
Yes. You can use Jardis in any commercial product, SaaS application, internal tool, or paid service. No license purchase is required.

**What counts as "competing" with Jardis?**
Building and providing a PHP framework, DDD scaffolding or code generation system, or DDD developer productivity tooling that serves the same purpose as Jardis. If you build an application *on top of* Jardis – a webshop, a CRM, a booking platform – that is not competition.

**Can I modify Jardis for my own use?**
Yes. You can make changes and build new works based on Jardis for any permitted purpose.

**Can I distribute my modified version of Jardis?**
Yes, provided your distribution includes the PolyForm Shield License terms and the required copyright notice, and your distribution does not compete with the Jardis platform.

**Can I fork Jardis and build a competing framework?**
No. That is the one restriction of this license. If you are interested in building developer tooling based on Jardis, contact us about partnership or OEM options.

**I am a freelancer working for a client. Does my client need a license?**
No. There is no separate commercial license to purchase. Your client can use Jardis freely, as long as they are not building a competing framework or developer tooling product.

**What happens if Jardis is discontinued?**
The PolyForm Shield License includes a discontinued products clause. If Headgent Development permanently stops providing the Jardis platform, the competition restriction lifts and the community is free to continue development without limitation.

**Is Jardis open source?**
Jardis is source-available. The PolyForm Shield License is not recognized as an open-source license by the OSI, because it includes a competition restriction. However, for the vast majority of use cases, Jardis is as permissive as a BSD or MIT licensed project.
